Study: Translanguaging and Trans-semiotizing in English-Medium Classrooms

📊Research Approach

Qualitative Case Study Design

A single case embedded design with two sub-units (educators and students) to explore complex phenomena in real-world context.

"A case study design was adopted for its capacity to explore a complex phenomenon 'in depth and within its real-world context'"
- Yin, 2018; Creswell & Poth, 2017

🏫Case Selection

Private University in Dhaka, Bangladesh

Selected as a typical and representative case of Bangladeshi private higher education with EMI policy.

"The focal university was chosen because it resembled other private universities in its EMI policy and practice and was an 'information-rich [case] for in-depth study'"
- Patton, 2015, p. 264

🎯Research Questions

  1. What ideologies do educators and students hold regarding English as the MOI?
  2. How do they perceive translanguaging in EMI classrooms?
  3. What are their actual language practices in drawing on English-only and translanguaging resources?

🏢Institutional Context

  • Established: 1993
  • Students: 8,704 (undergraduate & postgraduate)
  • Faculty: 467 full-time members
  • International Experience: 50%+ faculty with overseas background
  • Curriculum: North American Liberal Arts Model

Semi-structured Interviews

Individual Interviews

Duration: 25-35 minutes each

Language: English

Focus: Perceptions of EMI policy and translanguaging views

Classroom Observations

12

Lessons Observed

Duration: 80 minutes each

Type: Non-participant observation

Focus: Language practices and negotiation

Stimulated Recall

6

Educator Interviews

Timing: Immediately after observations

Method: Audio clips + field notes

Focus: Reasoning behind language practices

Phase 1: Initial Interviews

Semi-structured interviews with all participants to collect baseline data on EMI perceptions and translanguaging attitudes.

Phase 2: Classroom Observations

Two consecutive 80-minute lessons per educator observed and recorded with detailed field notes.

Phase 3: Stimulated Recall

Immediate post-observation interviews using audio clips to explore reasoning behind observed practices.

Phase 4: Data Triangulation

Multiple data sources combined for comprehensive understanding of language beliefs and practices.

"Data were collected from multiple sources, including semi-structured interviews with educators and students, stimulated-recall interviews with educators, and classroom observations. The use of multiple data sources would allow triangulation of our findings"
- Data Collection Section

Thematic Analysis Process

1

Transcription & Familiarization

Audio-recorded interviews transcribed by first author to familiarize with data. Member checks conducted for accuracy.

2

Multiple Readings & Coding

Multiple readings of transcriptions, examination for important meanings, compilation according to research questions.

3

Open Coding

Initial open coding confirmed through repeated reading and iterative comparisons of data.

4

Theme Development

Validated codes categorized into broader meaning units (themes) through constant comparison and relationship mapping.

5

Theme Refinement

Identified themes named, defined, and supported with relevant data extracts for reporting.

6

Classroom Observation Analysis

Integration of lesson transcriptions with field notes to identify translanguaging episodes and language practices.

Analysis Framework

  • García & Li (2014): Translanguaging conception
  • Creese & Blackledge (2010): Pedagogical translanguaging operationalization
  • Canagarajah (2018) & Lin (2019): Multimodal and trans-semiotic practices
"A thematic analysis was conducted on the interview data to identify language beliefs, perceptions and practices. Following recommendations made by Nowell et al. (2017), peer debriefing was employed during the process of developing initial codes and generating themes."

研究：英语媒介教学课堂中的跨语实践与跨符号化

📊研究方法

质性案例研究设计

采用单一案例嵌入式设计，包含两个子单元（教育者和学生），以探索真实世界情境中的复杂现象。

"采用案例研究设计是因为其能够'深入探索真实世界情境中的复杂现象'"
- Yin, 2018; Creswell & Poth, 2017

🏫案例选择

孟加拉国达卡私立大学

选择作为孟加拉国私立高等教育EMI政策的典型代表案例。

"选择该重点大学是因为它在EMI政策和实践方面与其他私立大学相似，是'深入研究的信息丰富案例'"
- Patton, 2015, p. 264

🎯研究问题

  1. 教育者和学生对英语作为教学媒介持有什么意识形态？
  2. 他们如何看待EMI课堂中的跨语实践？
  3. 他们在使用纯英语和跨语资源方面的实际语言实践是什么？

课堂观察

12

观察课程

持续时间：每次80分钟

类型：非参与式观察

重点：语言实践和协商

刺激回忆

6

教育者访谈

时机：观察后立即进行

方法：音频片段 + 实地笔记

重点：语言实践背后的推理

第一阶段：初始访谈

与所有参与者进行半结构化访谈，收集关于EMI认知和跨语态度的基线数据。

第二阶段：课堂观察

每位教育者连续两节80分钟的课程被观察和记录，并做详细的实地笔记。

第三阶段：刺激回忆

观察后立即进行访谈，使用音频片段探索观察到的实践背后的推理。

第四阶段：数据三角验证

结合多个数据源，全面理解语言信念和实践。

"数据从多个来源收集，包括与教育者和学生的半结构化访谈、与教育者的刺激回忆访谈以及课堂观察。使用多个数据源可以对我们的发现进行三角验证"
- 数据收集部分

主题分析过程

1

转录与熟悉

第一作者转录录音访谈以熟悉数据。进行成员检查以确保准确性。

2

多次阅读与编码

多次阅读转录文本，检查重要含义，根据研究问题进行汇编。

3

开放编码

通过重复阅读和数据的迭代比较确认初始开放编码。

4

主题发展

通过持续比较和关系映射，将验证的代码分类为更广泛的意义单元（主题）。

5

主题完善

识别的主题被命名、定义，并用相关数据摘录支持以供报告。

6

课堂观察分析

将课程转录与实地笔记整合，识别跨语情节和语言实践。

分析框架

  • García & Li (2014): 跨语概念化
  • Creese & Blackledge (2010): 教学跨语操作化
  • Canagarajah (2018) & Lin (2019): 多模态和跨符号实践
"对访谈数据进行主题分析以识别语言信念、认知和实践。根据Nowell等人（2017）的建议，在开发初始代码和生成主题的过程中采用了同行汇报。"
- 数据分析部分
