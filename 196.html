The Personal Relationship Frame in Love Fraud

Based on research by Pamela Faber (Applied Corpus Linguistics, 2025)

Research Overview

This research analyzed love fraud events within the context of the PERSONAL_RELATION and INTENTIONAL_DECEPTION frames in FrameNet. The study focused specifically on RELATIONSHIP - examining its stages, participants, and dimensions through corpus analysis.

The data consisted of extended conversations between 83 scammers and the researcher, recorded from January 2021 to June 2024. When analyzed on the SketchEngine platform, the collocates of "relationship" with the highest LogDice scores were identified and structured.

"Love fraud or romance scamming is a form of online deception that has become increasingly widespread in today's digital world. It is a variant of the 419 advance fee scam, in which fraudsters contact potential victims, via Internet, with an extremely lucrative offer. The victim is then asked to pay various fees upfront to receive a veritable fortune. However, after payment, the promised funds never materialize, and the scammer disappears with the victim's money."
- Faber (2025), p.1

Fraudster Personas & Historical Context

Historical Context of Love Fraud

Love fraud is not a new phenomenon. The oldest version is the Spanish Prisoner, dating back to the 1500s:

"In this scam, the target is approached by a trickster, who claims to be in correspondence with a wealthy English nobleman, held captive in Spain. The trickster offers the victim the possibility of paying some of the costs of the captive nobleman's release. In return, he not only promises the victim a percentage of the reward, but also the hand of the prisoner's beautiful daughter."
- Faber (2025), p.1

Modern versions are now conducted online, proliferating as more people use dating apps and social networking sites to search for romance.

The Evolution of Love Fraud Relationships

The research reveals that fraudsters methodically progress through specific relationship stages to build trust and commitment, ultimately leading to financial requests:

Stage 1: Friendship
Building initial trust and connection
  • Initiator: Friend request with complimentary message
  • Key Terms: genuine friendship, true friendship, good friendship
  • Common Scripts: "I will appreciate us being good friends in sincerity, honesty and trust."
  • Values Emphasized: honesty, sincerity, trust, truthfulness
  • Goal: Establish foundation of trust that will be leveraged later
Stage 2: Soulmateship
Deepening emotional connection
  • Transition: "Destiny brought us together"
  • Key Terms: soulmate, destiny, fate, future together
  • Common Scripts: "When it comes to marriage, there's actually nothing superior than being with your soul mate."
  • Timeframe: Rapid progression from friendship
  • Goal: Create sense of cosmic connection and destiny
Stage 3: Engagement
Formal commitment prior to crisis
  • Role Change: Victim becomes "fianc√©e"
  • Key Terms: fianc√©, fianc√©e, proposal, engagement
  • Functionality: Enables victim to authorize actions and payments
  • Common Examples: "You are writing on behalf of your fianc√© for an immediate vacation..."
  • Goal: Establish authority for victim to act on fraudster's behalf
Stage 4: Marriage
Linguistically creating spousal commitment
  • Linguistic Shift: Begins calling victim "wife"
  • Key Terms: husband, wife, marriage
  • Common Scripts: "May you have a merry and cheerful day, my beautiful wife."
  • Financial Implication: Spousal obligation to help financially
  • Goal: Create obligation that precedes financial requests
"The results show that fraudsters use scripts to construct a romantic relationship with victims, which begins with friendship, progresses to 'soulmateship' and engagement, and finally ends in an online 'marriage'. This is accomplished through the strategic use and repetition of terms that belong to the PERSONAL_RELATION frame in FrameNet. The objective is to extract as much money as possible from the victim."
- Faber (2025), Abstract

"In love fraud, a relationship is triggered by a cosmic force, or higher power (God, Fate, or Destiny) that has caused the paths of the fraudster and victim to cross. Both destiny and fate are terms that appeal to the human desire to believe that events do not randomly occur, and that everything happens for a reason."

This attribution to cosmic forces serves multiple strategic purposes:

  • Creates sense of special meaning in the relationship
  • Suggests relationship is beyond human control
  • Implies resistance would be fighting destiny
  • Establishes relationship as part of a "greater design"

Emotional Impact & Victim Profile

Emotional Impact on Victims

"In love fraud, the emotional loss is much worse than the loss of money because victims often mourn their phantom love long after their finances have recovered. According to Aborisade et al. (2024), love fraud victims are prone to suffer great psychological pain (shame, anxiety, depression, or even suicidal ideation)."
- Faber (2025), p.2

The research indicates several key factors that make love fraud particularly devastating:

  • Psychological Pain: Victims experience shame, anxiety, depression, and sometimes suicidal ideation
  • Prolonged Mourning: Many victims continue to mourn their "phantom love" long after financial recovery
  • Difficulty Releasing Attachment: Because victims never met the fraudster, they often find it difficult to erase their affection for the idealized image
  • Romantic Illusion: The success of the fraud lies in making victims believe the relationship is real through language and frame construction

Typical Victim Profile

"Typical female victims are well-educated middle-aged women, who are prone to romantic idealization. Precisely because they have never met the fraudster, they find it difficult to erase their affection for his idealized image even after they become aware of the deception."
- Faber (2025), p.2

The research identifies key characteristics of typical victims:

  • Demographics: Well-educated, middle-aged women
  • Psychological Tendency: Prone to romantic idealization
  • Religious Background: Often pious and family-oriented
  • Situation: May be seeking connection after loss or loneliness

Fraudsters specifically seek victims with these traits as they are more likely to respond positively to requests for financial support, especially when framed as helping a committed partner.

Research Contributions & Implications

Key Research Contributions

  • Frame Semantics Application: Applied Frame Semantics to analyze love fraud within PERSONAL_RELATIONSHIP and INTENTIONAL_DECEPTION frames
  • Corpus Analysis: Identified lexical patterns and semantic co-occurrence with quantitative evidence
  • Script Identification: Documented repetitive scripts used across different fraudsters
  • Relationship Stage Mapping: Identified clear progression through friendship, soulmateship, engagement, and marriage phases
  • Linguistic Signaling: Revealed how language constructs false relationships and creates obligation

Practical Implications

  • Automated Detection: Findings could enhance machine-learning methods for love fraud detection
  • Public Awareness: Helps raise awareness of linguistic patterns in love fraud
  • Prevention Tools: Could be applied to content-based detection of deceptive discourse
  • Education: Provides valuable information for potential victims on warning signs
  • Law Enforcement: Offers linguistic patterns that may assist in investigation and prosecution
"These semantic co-occurrence patterns and lexical regularities not only empirically support the results of other researchers on the language of love fraud but could also add to the resources used by Suarez-Tangil et al. (2020), who have worked on developing automated detection systems using machine-learning methods."
- Faber (2025), p.9
