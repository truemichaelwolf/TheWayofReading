🏛️ Key Policy Implications
1. Shift from English-only to Bi/Multilingual Policies

The study reveals a critical need for policy change from strict English-only mandates to more inclusive bi/multilingual approaches in Bangladeshi higher education.

"In this light, there is a need for a policy shift from an English-only to a bi/multilingual focus in Bangladeshi higher education."
- Study Conclusion
2. Recognition of Translanguaging as Legitimate Pedagogy

Educational policies should acknowledge translanguaging as a legitimate pedagogical practice rather than viewing it as deviation from EMI standards.

"Translanguaging and trans-semiotizing were an integral part of instruction for various epistemological and pedagogical reasons."
- Key Finding
3. Bridge Policy-Practice Gap

There is substantial disconnect between translingual practices in classrooms and monolingual ideologies in policymaking that requires systematic addressing.

4. Teacher Training and Support

Professional development programs should prepare educators to strategically use translanguaging for pedagogical purposes while maintaining academic standards.

5. Assessment Policy Reform

Evaluation frameworks should accommodate multilingual competencies and recognize translanguaging as a cognitive and pedagogical resource.
