Detailed Analysis of Research Methods and Considerations

Phase 1: Framework for GM Analysis

"When identifying GMs in these corpora, it is important to clarify that we were only interested in identifying experiential GMs, a subset of the broader linguistic phenomenon."

Key Considerations:

  • Focus on experiential GMs only
  • Based on Halliday and Matthiessen's typology
  • Simplified for pedagogical use

Omitted Categories:

  • Minor process to process shifts
  • Emergent categories of GM
  • Secondary shifts in GM syndromes

Phase 2: GM Identification Process

Step 1: Semantic Junction Analysis

"To identify GMs within these categories, we began by questioning whether a term possessed a sense of semantic junction."

Step 2: Derivation Test

"Derivation is indicated by the presence of derivational suffixes, such as -ment or -ness."

Step 3: Agnation Test

"Agnation involves the identification of a congruent form that precedes the GM."

Phase 3: Corpus Analysis

Corpus Analysis Method Key Considerations
Student Corpus (SSC) Manual Analysis 80 essays, Distinction/High Distinction grade
Research Article Corpus (RAC) Combined Automated & Manual 200 published research articles
"Analysis of a sample drawn from the Successful Student Corpus (SSC) was manual. This sample consisted of 80 essays, each of which was awarded a Distinction or High Distinction grade at an English-medium undergraduate level."

Phase 4: Verification and Refinement

Verification Steps:

  1. Manual checking of automated results
  2. Removal of false positives
  3. Identification of additional GMs
  4. Cross-validation between corpora
"The second function of the manual analysis was to supplement the GML. All GMs identified in the manual analysis of the RAC that had not featured in the SSC were then added to the data."
