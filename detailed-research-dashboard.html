Evaluating a Bracketing Protocol for Multiword Terms
多词术语括号协议评估

Research Questions & Objectives 研究问题与目标

"This paper presents a protocol that combines various models and applies it to a set of three-constituent MWTs in order to: (i) sort rules by their disambiguation potential, based on their likelihood of retrieving results from any corpus and their ability to solve bracketing; and (ii) ascertain the influence of corpus size and type in the results obtained."

Primary Research Questions:

  • How effective are different bracketing rules in disambiguating multiword terms?
  • What impact do corpus size and type have on bracketing results?
  • How can existing bracketing models be combined for optimal results?

Research Motivation 研究动机

Current Challenges:

  • Complex constituent relations in scientific texts
  • Translation accuracy depends on correct bracketing
  • Existing NLP models have limitations
  • Manual bracketing is time-consuming

Key Example:

"reactive power consumption could have various translations in Spanish, depending on whether the head of the term is power consumption, modified by reactive, or consumption, modified by reactive power."

Methodology 研究方法

Corpora Details:

Corpus Size Type
WPC 3,025,237 words Domain-specific
DOAJ 2,662,763,697 words General scientific

Protocol Components:

  • 12 Bracketing indicators
  • 34 CQL queries
  • 16 Bracketing rules
  • 103 Three-word MWTs analyzed

Results 研究结果

Overall Protocol Performance:

  • WPC Accuracy: 86.4%
  • DOAJ Accuracy: 79.6%
  • Left Bracketing Success Rate: Higher than right bracketing

Most Reliable Rules:

Rule Accuracy Reliability
Adjacency 86.4% High
Longer MWTs 83.5% High
Dependency 76.7% Medium

Discussion & Implications 讨论与启示

Key Findings:

  • Domain-specific corpus (WPC) outperformed larger general corpus
  • Three main rules sufficient for accurate bracketing
  • Surface patterns more effective in larger corpora
  • Protocol effectiveness varies with bracketing direction

Future Research Directions:

  • Explore left/right bracketing trends
  • Analyze failed cases in detail
  • Investigate impact of domain specificity
  • Study multidimensionality effects

"The approach to bracketing presented and analyzed in this paper is expected to reduce many of the problems or difficulties in MWT translation, as well as other related tasks, derived from a misunderstanding of the internal dependencies in MWTs."
