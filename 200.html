Pedagogical Implications

This research combining quantitative and qualitative studies yielded six important pedagogical implications for language teaching.

1. Unique Motivational Trajectories

Each individual's motivational trajectory is unique and one-of-a-kind, like a fingerprint. This underscores the importance of maintaining flexibility in lesson schedules.

"The 26 graphs for the 26 students in Figure 3 exhibit a wide range of shapes composed of straight, angular, zigzagging, intersecting, or overlapping lines, leaving an impression similar to 26 unique fingerprints." - Sato & Fujita

Teaching Strategy: Leave the content of a few classes open so instructors can incorporate materials suggested by students at different times in each term.

2. Stability of Cultural Motivation

Changes in motivation levels were infrequent for "Anime/Manga" and "Japanese Culture", but more frequent for other types.

"Due to technological advancements, consuming 'Anime/Manga' and learning 'Japanese Culture' have been significantly facilitated through media, offering language learners the flexibility to immerse themselves as often as they desire, whenever they can." - Sato & Fujita

Teaching Strategy: Integrate excerpts from students' favorite media materials such as movies, dramas, cooking shows, news, interactive games, and apps into assignments to sustain language learners' motivations.

3. Impact of Human Interactions

Language learners' motivations are greatly impacted by human interactions, including accidental encounters, communicative interactions with native speakers, peers, and family.

Teaching Strategy: Provide interactive communicative tasks and collaborative learning opportunities, both inside and outside the classroom, and engage students in critical thinking about sociocultural norms.

4. Varied Assessment Methods

Even highly motivated students may not always excel in written tests, especially with complex languages such as Japanese.

"Exam scores should not overshadow a student's ability to effectively understand and use language patterns." - Student 5 (Discussion board contribution, 4/29/2023)

Teaching Strategy: Use varied assessment methods to holistically evaluate language learners' capabilities and potential, including contingent oral interaction tests.

5. Real-world Language Opportunities

Opportunities such as trips to Japan and graduate study in Japan can positively influence language learners' motivation.

Teaching Strategy: Make students aware of beneficial opportunities such as field trips, study abroad options, cultural festivals, research projects, student club activities, business internship opportunities, and teaching assistant positions.

6. Personal Transformation

Language learners' motivations frequently emerge in tandem with their personal transformation.

Teaching Strategy: Provide students with ample opportunities to reflect on their learning progress and understand its impact on their personal growth, such as through weekly journals to express feelings about learning progress and future aspirations.

教学启示

这项结合定量和定性研究的研究为语言教学提供了六个重要的教学启示。

1. 独特的动机轨迹

每个人的动机轨迹都是独特的，如同指纹。这强调了在课程安排中保持灵活性的重要性。

"26名学生的26个图表呈现出由直线、角形、曲折、交叉或重叠线组成的各种形状，给人留下类似26个独特指纹的印象。" - 佐藤与藤田

教学策略：保留几节课的内容开放，以便教师在每学期不同时间纳入学生建议的材料。

2. 文化动机的稳定性

"动漫/漫画"和"日本文化"的动机水平变化不频繁，但其他类型的变化更频繁。

"由于技术进步，通过媒体消费'动漫/漫画'和学习'日本文化'已经得到显著便利，为语言学习者提供了灵活性，让他们可以随时随地沉浸其中。" - 佐藤与藤田

教学策略：将学生喜爱的媒体材料（如电影、戏剧、烹饪节目、新闻、互动游戏和应用程序）摘录融入作业中。

3. 人际互动的影响

语言学习者的动机受到人际互动的极大影响，包括偶然相遇、与母语者的交流互动、同伴和家人。

教学策略：提供交互式沟通任务和协作学习机会，在教室内外都有，并让学生对社会文化规范进行批判性思考。

4. 多样化的评估方法

即使是高度积极的学生也可能不总是在笔试中表现出色，尤其是对于像日语这样的复杂语言。

"考试分数不应掩盖学生有效理解和使用语言模式的能力。" - 学生5（讨论板贡献，2023年4月29日）

教学策略：使用多样化的评估方法全面评估语言学习者的能力和潜力，包括语境口语互动测试。

5. 真实世界的语言机会

诸如前往日本旅行和在日本研究生学习等机会可以积极影响语言学习者的动机。

教学策略：让学生了解有益的机会，如实地考察、留学选择、文化节、研究项目、学生俱乐部活动、商业实习机会和助教职位。

6. 个人转变

语言学习者的动机经常与个人转变同步出现。

教学策略：为学生提供充分的机会反思其学习进展并了解其对个人成长的影响，例如通过每周日记表达对学习进展和未来抱负的感受。

Research Overview

This study tracked the changes in motivation levels among 26 students of Japanese at a university in the United States during the 2022–2023 academic year. The researchers used a mixed-methods approach, combining quantitative surveys with qualitative interviews to understand the complex nature of language learners' motivations.

"Building on the concept of subjectivity, which is shaped through communicative interactions (Kramsch 2009), and the concept of translanguaging that extends to an individual's subjectivity (Li 2023), we propose viewing language learners' motivation as a constituent of their subjectivity, which ecologically emerges, develops, or changes through communicative interactions, enabled by translanguaging practices." - Sato & Fujita

Theoretical Framework

The researchers propose viewing language learners' motivation as part of their subjectivity, which is shaped through communicative interactions in social contexts. This ecological framework recognizes the bidirectional relationships among:

  • Language learners' subjectivities (including motivation)
  • Communicative interactions
  • Social contexts embedded in sociocultural environments

研究概述

本研究追踪了2022-2023学年美国一所大学26名日语学习者的动机水平变化。研究者采用混合研究方法，结合定量调查和定性访谈，以理解语言学习者动机的复杂性。

"基于通过交际互动形成的主体性概念（Kramsch 2009）和扩展到个体主体性的跨语言概念（Li 2023），我们提出将语言学习者的动机视为其主体性的组成部分，这种主体性通过交际互动在生态上产生、发展或变化，由跨语言实践促成。" - 佐藤与藤田

理论框架

研究者提出将语言学习者的动机视为其主体性的一部分，这种主体性通过社会环境中的交际互动形成。这一生态框架认识到以下方面之间的双向关系：

  • 语言学习者的主体性（包括动机）
  • 交际互动
  • 嵌入在社会文化环境中的社会背景
