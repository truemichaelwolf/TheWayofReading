Research: Translanguaging and Trans-semiotizing in English-Medium Classrooms

🎯 Research Questions: Strategic Formulation
1
RQ1: What ideologies do educators and students hold regarding English as the MOI of the university?
🧠 Strategic Logic: Addresses the "beliefs" component of Spolsky's framework, establishing baseline attitudes that drive policy acceptance or resistance
2
RQ2: How do educators and students perceive the role of translanguaging in the EMI classroom?
🧠 Strategic Logic: Bridges ideologies and practices by exploring attitudes toward multilingual pedagogy, revealing potential conflicts between policy and pedagogical beliefs
3
RQ3: What are the actual language practices and choices of educators and students in drawing on English-only and translanguaging/transsemiotizing resources in the classroom?
🧠 Strategic Logic: Captures observable behaviors to identify policy-practice gaps and validate theoretical framework through empirical evidence

🧠 理论框架证成
📊 Spolsky三分模型

组成部分：语言意识形态、管理和实践

"根据Spolsky（2004），语言政策包括'社区或社会的所有语言信念、实践和管理决策'"
- 理论基础
🎯 选择此理论的原因：

为多层次分析EMI和理解政策-实践差距提供全面框架，通过系统检查信念、管理和实际行为

🔄 跨语理论

核心概念：流畅使用完整语言储备

"跨语是指'使用说话者的完整语言储备，不拘泥于对社会和政治定义的命名语言边界的谨慎遵守'"
- Otheguy等，2015
🎯 选择此理论的原因：

解决EMI课堂中多语实践的现实，挑战单语意识形态，为理解自然语言行为提供框架

🎭 跨符号化视角

扩展：从语言到多模态资源

"跨符号视角通过分析其与各种符号资源（如手势和身体动作）的关系来扩展意义建构过程"
- Lin，2019
🎯 选择此理论的原因：

捕捉课堂交流中超越口语使用的全部复杂性，认识到实际教学和学习过程的多模态性质

🧠 Theoretical Framework Justification
📊 Spolsky's Tripartite Model

Components: Language ideologies, management, and practices

"According to Spolsky (2004), language policy comprises 'all the language beliefs, practices, and management decisions of a community or society'"
- Theoretical Foundation
🎯 Why This Theory:

Provides comprehensive framework for analyzing EMI at multiple levels and understanding policy-practice gaps through systematic examination of beliefs, management, and actual behaviors

🔄 Translanguaging Theory

Core Concept: Fluid use of full linguistic repertoire

"Translanguaging refers to the 'deployment of a speaker's full linguistic repertoire without regard for watchful adherence to the socially and politically defined boundaries of named languages'"
- Otheguy et al., 2015
🎯 Why This Theory:

Addresses reality of multilingual practices in EMI classrooms, challenging monolingual ideologies and providing framework for understanding natural language behaviors

🎭 Trans-semiotizing Perspective

Expansion: Beyond linguistic to multimodal resources

"The trans-semiotic perspective expands the process of meaning-making by analysing its relationship with various semiotic resources, such as gestures and bodily movements"
- Lin, 2019
🎯 Why This Theory:

Captures full complexity of classroom communication beyond verbal language use, recognizing multimodal nature of actual teaching and learning processes

🎯 Research Gap Identification Strategy

Authors systematically build case for research necessity through multiple gap identification techniques:

🌍 Geographical Gap

Bangladesh as "underrepresented in international scholarship on language policy" - creates opportunity for unique contribution to global knowledge base

🔬 Theoretical Gap

"Although extant research has explored translanguaging and EMI separately, there is little inquiry into translanguaging in higher education EMI contexts" - justifies theoretical integration

⚖️ Policy-Practice Gap

"Despite significant interventions to implement EMI, there is misalignment between the overarching policy objectives, ideological beliefs, and actual language practices" - creates empirical necessity

📊 Methodological Gap

"Only a handful of studies have examined micro-level language practices in the Bangladeshi EMI context, and their findings are mixed and inconclusive" - justifies new investigation

🔍 Variable Selection Logic
Language Ideologies
🎯 Theoretical Rationale: "Language ideologies are the values assigned to languages and language use, that is, what people think should be done"
📏 Measurement Approach: Semi-structured interviews exploring beliefs about EMI and translanguaging to capture belief systems
Language Practices
🎯 Theoretical Rationale: "Language practices refer to individuals' observable language behaviours and actual language choices"
📏 Measurement Approach: Classroom observations and stimulated recall interviews to capture actual behaviors vs. intentions
Translanguaging Instances
🎯 Theoretical Rationale: "Translanguaging is a valuable concept for language policy scholars to describe the linguistic situations"
📏 Measurement Approach: Analysis of classroom interactions and code-switching patterns to identify multilingual practices
Trans-semiotizing Practices
🎯 Theoretical Rationale: Recognition that "translanguaging encompasses not only linguistic resources but also semiotic resources"
📏 Measurement Approach: Analysis of gestures, visual aids, and multimodal communication in classroom settings
